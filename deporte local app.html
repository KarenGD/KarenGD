Context

Often, many sports enthusiasts face the challenge of connecting with other fans in their local area, making it difficult to form groups to enjoy sports activities together. This may result in a lack of motivation, frustration, and an obstacle for those who want to engage in some type of sport or recreational activity, especially when the activity requires a minimum number of people to participate.

Role

As a UX/UI designer, I played a key role in creating an intuitive and user-centered experience for the application. My responsibilities included conducting user research, developing wireframes and prototypes, and ensuring the visual design aligned with the overall brand and user needs. Also, I applied Material Design 3 guidelines, created smooth user journeys, and refined the app's information architecture to facilitate smooth navigation and interaction.

OBJECTIVE

To facilitate the connection between people passionate about sports according to their geographic location, while also encouraging, promoting, and creating a community around the practice of various sports and recreational activities.

SOLUTION

To connect through an application people passionate about different sports practices who are interested in organizing meet-ups and attending events and competitions to participate in their preferred sports. The app will allow: The interaction between one or more users for the purpose of participating in recreational and/or sports activities. The creation of events and sports competitions based on the users' locations. The creation of a profile for each user that provides the necessary information to facilitate user growth and interaction based on their preferences.

Unique Value Proposition

Provide a space for interaction through an application that allows users to connect and interact for the participation in or creation of sports activities, events, and competitions based on their geographic location.

Card sorting

Once the MVP process is completed, the terms related to the application are compiled for users to categorize and group them in order to create the information architecture within the app.

Similarity
Matrix

As shown in the similarity matrix, participants identified 100% of the terms within a category related to sports. They mostly categorized time-related terms concerning days of the week and months. Participants created a more varied classification for personal data and terms like athlete, enthusiast, participant, etc. Finally, there were two terms that caused confusion during the activity, as they were not clearly related to other words: "weekends" and "competition."

Information Architecture

Based on the previous stages, the information architecture for the application was developed. Adjustments were made to the naming of categories to provide greater clarity regarding the functionality of each page and the information that will be presented to the user in the app.

TASKFLOW

Two task flows were proposed according to the needs of each user: one for those looking to participate in a sports activity or find other people, and the other, for those users who want to create a sports event.

Usability Testing

For the usability testing of the application, each participant was asked to perform the following tasks:

  • Participate in a volleyball practice
  • Create a sports event
  • Create their respective profile

All of this was done to evaluate the functionality, ease of navigation, and user experience of the app.

Test Considerations

What Worked:

100% of the users who participated in the test successfully completed each of the tasks set out for the test. 4 out of 5 users showed strong interest in the application and expressed a desire to learn more about it in the future.

What Did
Not Work:

The prototype had issues with form inputs and their interaction with the keyboard, causing frustration among users during testing. Some users tried to create their profile in the top bar, although this functionality was designed to be in the bottom bar navigation.

Recommendations

It was noted that help features accompanying users through various functionalities were necessary in several instances. A recommendation that hadn't been considered was allowing users to edit fields they had already filled out. Improve the application's interactions with the correct functionalities.
